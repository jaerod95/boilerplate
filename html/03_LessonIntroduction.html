Unit 3

Transformations

Transformations happen when a geometric object is changed in some way while leaving in place most of its properties—in some cases, all of its properties. Transformation types include translation, reflection, rotation, and dilation. In translation, the figure is moved a certain distance in the horizontal and vertical direction. In reflection, a figure is transformed when it is re-created the same distance from a specific line. In rotation, a figure is turned a certain number of degrees about a specific point. In dilation, a figure is transformed when it is made bigger or smaller while leaving the same angles.

In this unit, we will examine each of these different transformations individually and then put them together to see if we can determine which transformations have been used.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Translate figures and express the translation in algebraic terms.
  2. Reflect figures over different lines and determine the reflection used.
  3. Rotate different figures and determine the rotation that occurred.
  4. Dilate figures from the origin and determine the dilations used.
  5. Use different transformations to move a figure to another location.
  6. Determine the transformations used to move a figure.
