Missing Angle Measures

In the previous lesson, we looked at two parallel lines cut by a transversal and saw that it created 8 angles.

When we know one of the angles, we can find all the rest of the angles. For example, if ˂ 1 is 140 degrees, then ˂ 3 is 40 degrees because ˂ 1 and ˂ 3 are a linear pair.

Examine the following chart to see the measures of all the angles.

Angle Measure Reason
1 140° given
2 40° linear pair with <1
3 40° linear pair with <1
4 140° vertical angle to <1
5 140° corresponding angle to <1
6 40° linear pair to <5
7 40° linear pair to <5
8 140° alternate exterior to <1

Note that there can be more than one reason to show the measure of a given angle.
