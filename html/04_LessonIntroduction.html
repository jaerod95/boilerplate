Unit 4

Similarity and Congruence

In this unit we will be looking at different types of figures that look the same. Figures are similar when they have the same angles but different lengths of their sides. Figures are congruent when they have the same lengths as well as the same angle measures. We saw similar figures when we performed dilations in the last unit, and the other transformations were all of congruent figures.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Use similar figures to find the missing measure of another figure.
  2. Determine if two figures are similar using transformations.
  3. Determine similarity of polygons not using transformations.
  4. Determine if two figures are congruent using transformations.
  5. Determine congruence of polygons not using transformations.
  6. Determine whether two figures are similar, congruent, or neither of these.
