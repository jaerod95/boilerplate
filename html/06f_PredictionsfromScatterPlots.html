One way of enhancing our predictions is to actually calculate the equation of the trend line. In order to do this, we need the slope and the y-intercept to put the line into slope-intercept form.

First we calculate the slope. Slope is calculated by taking two points that are on the trend line; they do not necessarily have to be points that we have measured. We will use (4,80) and (8,90).

This means that for every 2 hours of studying we do, our test score increases 5 points. The y-intercept is about 70.
