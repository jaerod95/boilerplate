Unit 1

Roots and the Pythagorean Theorem

Pythagoras was an ancient philosopher who found ratios in mathematics and a relationship with right triangles. He developed this relationship called the Pythagorean theorem . To understand the Pythagorean theorem, you need to also understand the meaning and use of radicals (roots).

In this unit, you will learn how to simplify roots and use this information to understand the Pythagorean theorem. You will also learn a proof of the Pythagorean theorem. The Pythagorean theorem is often used to find one side length of a triangle and to find distances when given other information.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Simplify roots using square and cube roots
  2. Find missing lengths of triangles using the Pythagorean theorem.
  3. Write and evaluate the proof for the Pythagorean theorem.
  4. Use the Pythagorean theorem to solve real-world problems.
  5. Use the Pythagorean theorem to find the distance between two points.
