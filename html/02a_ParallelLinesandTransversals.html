Parallel Lines and Transversals

Parallel lines are lines on the same plane that do not cross each other. Perpendicular lines are lines on the same plane that cross each other at a 90 degree angle.

Parallel lines

Perpendicular lines

When parallel lines are crossed by another line, that line is called a transversal.

When two parallel lines are cut by a transversal, it creates different angle relationships.

Corresponding Angles: <1 and <5, <2 and <6, <3 and <7, <4 and <8
Corresponding angles are congruent (they have equal measures).

Alternate Interior Angles: <3 and <6, <4 and <5
These angles are on the inside of the parallel lines, and on opposite sides of the transversal.
Alternate interior angles are congruent.

Alternate Exterior Angles: <1 and <8, <2 and <7
These angles are on the outside of the parallel lines, and on the opposite sides of the transversal. Alternate exterior angles are congruent.

When two angles form a straight line (a linear pair), they are supplementary. This means that the measures of their angles added together are 180 degrees. For example, <1 and <3 are supplementary.
