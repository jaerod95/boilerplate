Angles of Triangles

In a triangle, there are three angles on the inside of that triangle.
These angles are called interior angles.

In the example at the right, we have three angles: x, y, z.

If we were to add the measures of the interior angles of this triangle, it would total 180 degrees. In other words:

x + y + z = 180

Example 1

If the measures of two of the angles of a triangle were 20 degrees and 55 degrees, what would the measure of the third angle be?

We will call the measure of the third angle x.

20 + 55 + x = 180
75 + x = 180
x = 105

The measure of the third angle is 105 degrees.

Example 2

If the angles of a triangle are in a ratio of 2:3:5, what are the measures of the angles? Since we know the angles of a triangle add to 180 degrees:

2x + 3x + 5x = 180
10x = 180
x = 18

The angles of this triangle are 2(18) or 36 degrees, 3(18) or 54 degrees, and 5(18) or 90 degrees.

If we were to extend the lines of our triangle, we would make additional angles. These angles are called exterior angles; <1 is an exterior angle, and <2 is an interior angle.

Note that <1 and <2 form a linear pair. This means that <1 and <2 are supplementary, or in other words <1 + <2 = 180.

Example 3

Given a right triangle with one interior angle of 20 degrees as shown in the figure, find the measure of exterior angle x.

The sum of the interior angles of a triangle is 180 degrees. We can find the missing interior angle of the triangle as follows:

180 = 90 + 20 + y
180 = 110 + y
70 = y

Then, since x and y make a linear pair, x + y = 180. When we substitute in the value for y, x + 70 = 180. Thus, x = 110 degrees.
