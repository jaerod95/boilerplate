Unit 5

Volume

In this unit, we will examine what fills up a three-dimensional space. This “filler” is what we call the volume of an item. Whenever we want to figure out how much water is in a pool, how much paint is in a can, or how much air is in a room, we look at volume. Volume comes in a variety of shapes and sizes, so we will look at some formulas that help us to find the volume of the different sizes. Sometimes we have to combine different pieces in order to find the entire volume of an unusual shape.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Use formulas to find the area of circles and volume of cylinders.
  2. Use the formula to find the volume of cones.
  3. Use the formulas to find the volume of spheres and hemispheres.
  4. Find the volume of composite figures using formulas.
  5. Solve for the radius and height of cones, cylinders, and spheres.
