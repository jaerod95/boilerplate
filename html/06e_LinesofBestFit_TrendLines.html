Lines of Best Fit (Trend Lines)

One of the ways that we can make predictions on a set of bivariate data is to create a line of best fit on a scatter plot. A line of best fit (or trend line) is a line that can be drawn that is close to the data points that are actually given in a scatter plot. Look at the scatter plot that we created in lesson 6.3:

We then draw a line that is close to the data points that are given.

The line helps in showing us that it has a positive association, since the slope of the line is positive. We can also use the line to make predictions about what will happen. For example, we can predict that if we do no studying at all, we will get about a 70% on the test. We can also predict that if we study for 12 hours we will get a 100% on the test.
