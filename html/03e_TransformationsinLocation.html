Transformations in Location

Now that we have learned about translations, reflections, rotations, and dilations, we can move a particular figure anywhere we wish.

Example

Triangle ABC has vertices of A(3, 1), B(5, 2), and C(4, 4). Translate the figure down 2 and right 1, then reflect the figure over the x-axis. Finally, rotate the figure 90 degrees clockwise. Give the coordinates of the new triangle.

First we do the translation:

(x, y) → (x + 1, y −2
A (3,1) → A′(4,−1)
B (5,2) → B′(6,0)
C (4,4) → C′(5,2)

Next we do the reflection:

(x, y) → (x, −y)
A′(4, −1) → A′′(4,1)
B′(6,0) → B′′(6,0)
C′(5,2) → C′′(5, −2)

Finally we do the rotation:

(x, y) → (y, −x)
A′′(4,1) → A′′′(1, −4)
B′′(6,0) → B′′′(0, −6)
C′′(5, −2) → C′′′(−2, −5)
