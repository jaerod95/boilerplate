Pythagorean Theorem Proof

The Pythagorean theorem proof is used to demonstrate that the Pythagorean theorem works. The Pythagorean theorem starts with a right triangle, and then we prove that a2 + b2 = c2.

The proof uses a simple rearrangement of the components as drawings of squares, as shown in your reading assignment for this lesson. In this lesson you will create 8 copies of a right angle and compare the squares that you see as you arrange the right triangles. Follow the steps as shown in the investigation.

The converse of the Pythagorean theorem can also be proven. The converse is a reversal of the parts of the equation, so a2 + b2 = c2 becomes what we are given, and we are trying prove that if the equation works, then we have a right triangle.

Reading Assignment

Read pages 419–420 (Investigation 1) in the Glencoe Math textbook. Carefully go through each step of the investigation. The investigation will teach you how the Pythagorean theorem works and why it works.
