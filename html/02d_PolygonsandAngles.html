Polygons and Angles

A polygon can be split up into triangles. Since each triangle is 180 degrees, the sum of the interior angles of the polygon is found by multiplying the number of triangles that can be drawn from one vertex by 180. In the figure at the right, the number of triangles that can be drawn is 4. Thus, the sum of the interior angles is 4 (180), or 720 degrees.

If we define the number of sides of a polygon as n, and then the sum of the interior angles of a polygon as S, then we can represent the sum of the interior angles of a polygon by the equation:

S = (n − 2)180

Example 1

Find the sum of the interior angles of an octagon.

An octagon has 8 sides. So:

S = (8 − 2)180
S = (6)180
S = 1080

The sum of the interior angles of an octagon is 1080 degrees.

If we know we have a regular polygon, then all the sides and angles of the polygon are the same. So we can find the measure of one interior angle by taking the sum and dividing by the number of sides. In our example above we would take 1080 divided by 8 and get each angle equal to 135 degrees.

In a polygon, the sum of the measures of the exterior angles is equal to 360 degrees. In other words, <1 + <2 + <3 = 360 degrees.

This is true for any polygon, not just triangles.

Example 2

Find the measure of an exterior angle of a regular pentagon.

There are 5 sides on a pentagon, so there are 5 exterior angles that are all equal. If we let the measure of the exterior angle of a pentagon be x, then 5(x) = 360 or x = 72 degrees.

The measure of the exterior angle of a regular pentagon is 72 degrees.
