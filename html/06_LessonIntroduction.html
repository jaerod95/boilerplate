Lesson 6

Bivariate Data

Often, we want to look at and compare two different ideas. Bivariate means comparing two different ideas together—“bi” meaning two and “variate” meaning variables. In this unit, we will look at two models that can be used to examine bivariate data. First we will look at two-way tables and their relative frequencies, and then we will look at scatter plots and their lines of best fit in order to make predictions about the data.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Create and interpret two-way tables.
  2. Find and determine associations using relative frequencies.
  3. Construct and interpret scatter plots.
  4. Analyze distributions using outliers and other considerations.
  5. Determine the lines of best fit (trend lines) for a scatter plot.
  6. Make predictions using lines of best fit and trend lines.
